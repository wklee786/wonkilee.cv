About

I am a postdoctoral researcher focusing on merging galaxy clusters, radio relics, weak‑lensing mass reconstructions, and self‑interacting dark matter. My research combines large cosmological simulations with observational campaigns across radio, optical, and X‑ray wavelengths.

Projects

Reconstructing Cluster Merger Histories

Using radio relics as tracers of merger shocks, I developed methods to estimate time since collision and merger geometry. This work helps constrain dark matter models and plasma physics in extreme environments.

TNGC-SIDM Simulations

I lead the TNGC-SIDM project, simulating massive cluster mergers with self‑interacting dark matter physics and full galaxy formation models. These simulations provide the first cosmological dataset of this kind.

Multi-wavelength Observations

From MeerKAT radio surveys to XMM-Newton and JWST observations, I integrate diverse datasets to understand cluster merger shocks, diffuse radio emissions, and the role of baryonic physics in cosmic structure formation.
